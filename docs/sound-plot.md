# Hamlet Sound Plot - First Pass

This production wants restraint. The script already has a lot of emotional noise, so sound should usually do one of four jobs:

- establish place: castle night, court, churchyard
- mark the supernatural: the Ghost, purple light, poisoned memory
- support live theatrical action: rooster, door break, tray clatter, dagger/body moments
- carry transitions: scene changes, act endings, intermission, final image

## Sonic Palette

**Elsinore exterior:** cold air, sparse crickets, distant bird/crow/rooster, stone and sea suggested rather than literal.

**The Ghost:** low bowed metal, breath, reversed resonance, a little sub pressure. It should feel wounded and ancient, not like a haunted-house jump scare.

**The Court:** elegant but wrong. Lute/harp/viol-like material, polished rhythm, with small sour intervals underneath.

**Hamlet interior:** quiet pressure, dry room tone, small unstable pitch. Avoid scoring every soliloquy.

**Ophelia:** mostly live voice. Any playback should be a fragile pitch bed or water/flower texture, never a full sentimental song track unless rehearsal asks for it.

**The Duel:** controlled public ceremony that becomes machinery. Music can continue through the act, but it must stay thin enough for sword, breath, and text.

## Likely Live / Practical Instead Of Playback

- Ophelia's songs should be live first.
- Hamlet's harp should be live if Evan can do it reliably.
- Sword clashes should come from the weapons unless a specific beat is too quiet.
- Body falls and dragging Polonius should be practical unless rehearsal proves otherwise.
- The Clowns' digging can be practical with a hidden tray/box of soil or gravel.
